ME 2110 Robotics Competition

Summer 2023 | ME 2110

Arduino Rapid Prototyping 3D Printing SolidWorks

Introduction & Goal

  • ME 2110 is a robotics competition course at Georgia Tech where teams of 4 design and build a robot using mechatronics, pneumatics, and basic mechanics.
  • The class concludes with a competition and students have their robots compete with other teams.
  • The goal of the competition is to score the maximum number of points via manipulation of field objectives.

Field Layout

This was the final layout of the ME 2110 competition arena. Our robot had to push Walkers out of out quadrant, deliver torpedo balls into the rotating center piece, grab lightsabers, and finally displace Baby Yoda.

Technical Details

Important Constraints

  • 40 Second Run Time
  • 3.5 Minute Setup Time
  • 24" x 18" x 12" Bounding Box
  • < $100 Cost
  • No active actuators before or after round

Design Challenges

  • Mechanically extend and interact with field objectives.
  • Deliver torpedo balls in timed sequence.
  • Anticipate and design for any interaction from other teams (pushed walkers, sabers, misc. objects pushed into our quadrant).

Results

Ended up using

  • Drawer Slide as extension mechanism, with two servos: one for release and another for retraction
  • 2 pneumatically activated wedger arms
  • 2 Spring loaded "Walker" arms, released with the activation of the pneumatics
